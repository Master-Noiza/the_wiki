# Overview:
<br>

**The Gods**, whatever they truly are, sleeping, dead, removed, malevolent or altruistic, or even non-existent, are seen with a multitude of interpretations, prioritisation and reverence depending on culture, social standing or even momentary needs.
<br>
The Most common interpretations in the present day are the *"Hierarchy-Model"* and the *"Aspect-Model"*.

---
Hierarchy-Model:
- All Gods exist as individuals
- Mayor Gods rule over Minor Gods
- Minor Gods take responsibility over parts of the domain of their superiors

Aspect-Model:
- Only [[1. The Compendium/C_Gods & Religion/2. The One True God/1. The One\|The One]] exists as an individual
- All other "gods" are mere aspects, no individuals themselfes
- Some, most prominently The imperial [[1. The Compendium/D_The Races/F_The Urashalyr/index\|Urashalyr]], interpret [[1. The Trinity\|The Trinity]] as the truly existing triage of Gods where other proponents view them as primary aspects of The One 

---
In times before, despite their waning relevance today, the now so called "[[1. The Compendium/C_Gods & Religion/5. Minor Gods, Spirits & Aspects/index\|Minor Gods]]" where actually the primary gods worshipped.
<br>
Scrolls, paintings and engravements tell us, that in ancient times, as far back as the *Convergence* and before the rise of the *Younger Races* show almost exclusively widespread worship of *The Nine*, *The Eighteen* and *The Many*.
<br>
The vast majority of deities are neither purely "good", nor "evil". The only exceptions to this might be [[1. The Compendium/C_Gods & Religion/4. The Nine/D_The Bound One\|The Bound One]] and [[1. The Compendium/C_Gods & Religion/5. Minor Gods, Spirits & Aspects/B_The Gracious Reflection\|The Garcious Reflection]] respectively, and even their true motives, agendas and intentions might prove more nuanced then immediately obvious.
<br>
In general, all religions, faiths, cults and niche believes have temples, holidays and ideals the life by. It is a common superstition among the common folk though, that if one ever has to choose between worship and fear, they would be wise to choose fear.
<br>
Lastly, although primarily of scholarly interest and not on the minds of many of even the most zealous folk, the Gods we worship today are the young ones on a cosmic scale, the ones which wrestled control over reality from the primordials at the beginning of time.
